Lead generation: What is it?

Any person who expresses a specific interest in your brand can be categorised as a lead, regardless of their age,gender, or other pertinent traits.

The majority of the time, leads introduce themselves by giving you their contact information, engaging with your page, or following your blog. Keeping track of these behaviours is essential for your sales because any of their acts could be a clear sign of intent to buy.

Even if your social media sites or website development may be inundated with visitors or questions, keep in mind that not all of the leads you receive will be of the same calibre. Your prospective clients could be at various points in the sales funnel, and the majority of them aren't quite ready to seal the deal just yet. In fact, statistics reveal that 79% of marketing leads fail to result in sales, with a lack of nurturing being the main culprit.

Lead generation is then used in this situation. The process of attracting, fostering, and persuading potential clients to do business with you is known as lead generation. For instance, when a website visitor indicates a desire to make a purchase, marketers employ a variety of strategies to win that customer's business.

To offer you a few examples, email marketing and content marketing are both straightforward but effective techniques.

Creating a coordinated and efficient lead generation strategy is now essential for all businesses as the market has moved towards the digital sphere. Additionally, you must work twice as hard as before to outperform competitors' firms because the competition is more fierce than ever. Increasing your focus on lead creation will help your customer acquisition efforts, as well as strengthen brand loyalty and increase your revenue.

The Lead Generation Funnel

When buying something, each person has a particular technique that they use. All clients adhere to a set process that leads to the transaction—your end goal—despite these differences. The lead generation funnel was developed to assist marketers in visualising and identifying strategies to impact the customer experience.



The lead generation funnel depicts a customer's journey from the time they provide you their information until they buy your goods or services.



Top of the Funnel (TOFU) - Building awareness is the main focus of this phase. Since this is your first encounter with the guest, building trust should be your top concern. By providing them with timely and pertinent information, you may establish yourself as a knowledgeable and trustworthy source.



Middle of the Funnel (MOFU) - Your visitors are beginning to consider their alternatives and have already acknowledged the necessity to fix their issue in the middle of the funnel. You can move them down the conversion route by giving an excellent solution or something of value.



Bottom of the Funnel (BOFU) - You can already refer to leads as prospects once they have descended to the bottom of the funnel. Since they are in the consideration stage of the buying process, your primary goal should be to sell. They will feel more confident in choosing to do business with you if you provide them with case studies, demos, and price comparisons.



You'll be able to influence your leads into making purchases if you thoroughly comprehend the three stages. The lead generating funnel and email marketing is helpful for improving the consumer experience in addition to raising conversion chances.
